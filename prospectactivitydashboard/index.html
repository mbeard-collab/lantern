Benchmark coaching — activity required to qualify (tier-specific)

Benchmarks = the average calls + emails logged on qualified prospects in each tier (the effort it actually took to get a Yes). A closed prospect is Below if its activity is under 75% of its tier bar (risk of disqualifying too quickly), Above if over 125% (over-contacting / pest risk), else On. Percentages and the per-rep table reflect the current filters (set above) and cover closed prospects only (qualified + disqualified), excluding test/internal.

Methodology & anomaly notes

Calls come from Salesforce Task activity (TaskSubtype='Call') matched to each prospect's Contact. The prospect record's own rollup fields (Count_of_Engage_with_Prospect__c, meeting & email-outreach counts) were empty across all 1,016 prospects in the source pull — a data-integrity gap — so record-level counts were not used.

Emails come from the BigQuery export, matched by contact email, and are counted only within each prospect's open→close window (created date to close date, or to today for still-open prospects) using the per-email timestamps — sends before the record existed or after it closed are excluded from the activity count and the benchmark, and surfaced separately as anomalies. All-time emails to matched contacts: 2,539 (vs 1,236 in-window).

Open opportunities = count and sum(Amount) of Opportunity records with IsClosed=false on the prospect's related Account.

Anomaly logic: emails predating prospect creation; emails continuing >1 day after a prospect was disqualified/closed (cadence overrun); a contact email attached to more than one prospect record; test/internal records ("test" in name/company/email or company = GovSpend); and prospects with in-window email activity but zero logged calls.

Data provenance & refresh model. Current snapshot: 1,016 prospects, 729 calls, 1,236 in-window emails, 994/994 CSV emails matched (0 unmatched), 225 open opps, 10 test/internal excluded from benchmarks. Past activity is frozen — calls, emails and meetings that already occurred are immutable and are never recomputed; closed prospects are locked. Refreshes are append-only: new/changed prospects and any new activity are added to the snapshot and only still-open prospects are re-checked. No values are hard-coded — every number is derived from the snapshot at render time, and anything unavailable (empty SF rollups, tiers/reps with too few qualified prospects to set a bar) is labeled rather than estimated.
